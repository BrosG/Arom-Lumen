Our Story

The Art of Light & Wood

The Artist

Romain Davidico

A cabinetmaker and woodworker for over a dozen years, Romain Davidico now dedicates himself with passion to creating luminaires from wood. He learned to tame this living material, bending it to his vision through steam — a dialogue of patience and precision.

He blends essences and sculpts them into harmonious, weightless forms, balanced on the edge of the extraordinary — giving birth to decorative luminaires that cast soft, subdued, soothing atmospheres of light.

"I bend the wood to my desire, but always with respect. The material guides me as much as I shape it."

Every creation is a unique piece. No two lamps are alike — each carries the memory of the tree it once was, the marks of fire that revealed its soul, and the patient hands that gave it a second life as a vessel of light.

Our Philosophy

Three Truths of Wood

I

Respect the Grain

Every piece of wood carries a story written by decades of sun, rain, and wind. We never force a shape. The natural grain guides every cut.

II

Honor by Fire

Through Shou Sugi Ban, we use controlled flame to reveal the wood's hidden character — its deepest colors that only fire can unlock.

III

Light as Soul

Each lamp is designed so light doesn't merely pass through — it breathes. The glow becomes part of the sculpture, transforming space into sanctuary.

The Craft

From Forest to Form

01

Selection

Each piece of wood is personally chosen from sustainable forests for its grain, character, and story.

02

Vision

The wood speaks first. We study its natural curves, letting the material guide the design.

03

Crafting

Hand tools, steam bending, and Shou Sugi Ban — each technique reveals the wood's essence.

04

Finishing

Natural oils and hand-polishing create a living finish that grows more beautiful with time.
